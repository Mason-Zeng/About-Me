About Me

My name is Mason Zeng, and I am a 2nd year student at the Rochester Institute of Technology. I am from Brooklyn, New York, just a few hours south of a drive from Rochester. Here's a brief overview and timeline of my life. I grew up as an average kid going to public school starting from pre-school all the way to high school. I considered myself to be a “goody-two-shoes” as I always was following the rules and maintained high grades. However, I was very quiet and reserved and all I really liked to do during my middle school and early high school days was to play video games and nothing else. Looking back, the harm was not worth the “fun” as I quickly detached from my surroundings and essentially had no social skills. I am very thankful that I was able to find a group of people in my church that helped me to be more verbal and overall opened my eyes to the world.

Now that I am in college, I have even more opportunities to improve myself. College is the steppingstone to living independently where all the choices are up to the individual. Here I hope to learn how to live independently while also diving even deeper into my interests. For example, finding another Christian fellowship to better my faith, taking SWEN courses to better my knowledge in software engineering, and playing open gym volleyball to better my skills in volleyball.

My Hometown

I am from Brooklyn NY, one of the 5 boroughs in New York City. One thing I really like about Brooklyn is its variety of activities. Since it's located in the city, there are many good restaurants and sources of entertainment such as parks, museums, shops, culture towns, and so much more. Additionally, the public transportation system is very convenient as it can take you all around the city. Not only do I have access to Brooklyn activities but also activities within Manhattan, Queens, and the Bronx. However, there are some negatives. The city is very dangerous and has many crazy people. The crime rate is very high to the point where New York City is known throughout social media for its stereotypical uncouth behavior.

    • Course that focuses on the design of software and producing good code.
